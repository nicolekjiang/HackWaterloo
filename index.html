Rules

Participants must either begin working on a new project from scratch, or write an independent module for a larger piece of existing software.

If the latter is chosen, only the module itself will be taken into consideration for judging. When making a submission, the participant must also chose ‘module’ as their project type.

Judging Criterion

Beauty

Having a beautiful user interface will be considered equivalent to coming up with a elegant technical solution to an analytic problem.

Depth

Points will be awarded for scope of the project created or the problem solved within the 24 hour time period.

Rigor

Entries that show the most attention to detail or efficiency in solving a problem will score well in this category.

FAQ

What prizes are available?

The first-place winning team will receive $500 CAD to be divided amongst themselves. The runner-ups will receive $250 CAD.

What program do I have to be in?

Any student in any Waterloo program/faculty plan is welcome to participate in the hackathon. Graduate students are welcome as well.

Do I have to have prior programming experience?

Prior programming experience would be an asset. However, anyone who is motivated to learn, create and solve problems is encouraged to attend.

What should I bring?

You should bring a personal computing device, such as your laptop, to work on.

Must I have a team?

No. You are free to work on your own. Although, collaboration with other students is encouraged.

Will there be food provided at the event?

Unfortunately, we won’t be providing caffeine at the event. However, we will be providing snacks and meals to participants.
